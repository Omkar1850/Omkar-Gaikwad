FCA (Free Carrier)

Understanding Responsibilities, Risk Transfer, and Transportation under Incoterms® 2020

What is FCA?

FCA (Free Carrier) is an Incoterm® that defines the responsibilities of buyers and sellers during international trade. Under FCA, the seller delivers goods to a carrier nominated by the buyer at an agreed location. Once delivered, the risk transfers to the buyer.

Seller's Responsibilities

  • Prepare and package the goods.
  • Deliver goods to the carrier nominated by the buyer.
  • Pay costs up to the agreed delivery point.
  • Handle export customs clearance.
  • Provide export documentation.

Buyer's Responsibilities

  • Nominate a carrier for transport.
  • Arrange and pay main freight charges.
  • Arrange cargo insurance if required.
  • Handle import customs clearance.
  • Pay import duties and taxes.

Risk Transfer

Seller
Carrier
Buyer

Risk transfers from the seller to the buyer when the goods are handed over to the carrier at the agreed delivery location.

Key Points of FCA

Delivery Point

Goods are delivered to a carrier or another party nominated by the buyer.

Risk Transfer

Risk transfers when the goods are handed over to the carrier.

Transportation

Buyer is responsible for the main transportation and insurance.

Example Scenario

London ➜ Mumbai

A seller in London agrees to sell goods to a buyer in Mumbai under FCA London.

  • Seller delivers goods to the carrier in London.
  • Buyer arranges transport to Mumbai.
  • Buyer pays freight and insurance costs.
  • Buyer handles import duties and customs clearance.
  • Risk transfers once the carrier receives the goods.

Advantages of FCA

Flexible Delivery

Delivery can occur at various agreed locations.

Buyer Control

Buyer can choose the carrier and transport route.

Cost Transparency

Responsibilities are clearly divided between parties.

Versatile

Suitable for containerized and non-containerized cargo.
